Facilities

Invictus Woods

Nestled on 35 acres of serenely wooded property 20 miles away from Peoria, Illinois, Invictus Woods offers a clean, enjoyable and private sober living community for men. Invictus Woods has 5 bedrooms and 4 bathrooms, hardwood oak flooring in the dining room, kitchen and living room. The upstairs living room has a large TV, a telescope, and internet. The kitchen has a gas stove, commercial refrigerator, oven and microwave.

There are decks in the front and back of the home for relaxation and reflection, as well as central air conditioning throughout the house. On the lower level there is large group meeting room, two bedrooms, a bathroom, and a kitchen. The lower level walks out to a patio with a grill and scenic views of the woods. The local wildlife consists of birds, deer, fox, and turkey.

Prospect House

Prospect house has 5 bedrooms, which can house 8 male residents. There are 3 full bathrooms, 1 full kitchen on the main floor, 2 full living areas with internet and cable tv, laundry facilities and a front and back yard. The cleaning duties and cooking meals are shared by the residents to foster personal responsibility and community within the house. The rent is $150 a week. Prospect house is on a bus line, which is convenient for residents who need transportation for jobs and community events.

Circle House

Circle House is a sober living home for women 18 and older who are in recovery and have successfully completed at least a 28 day treatment program. The house has 4 bedrooms, 2 full bathrooms, a fully operating kitchen and 2 living rooms, as well as internet, cable TV and laundry facilities. There is a backyard with a privacy fence and a gas grill. Circle house is located a half block from a bus stop, close to grocery stores and job opportunities. The neighborhood is quiet and lovely. There are 2 residents to a room and the rent is $150 a week. We are a twelve step facility and require our residents to do 90 meetings in 90 days. We look forward to supporting you in this journey.
